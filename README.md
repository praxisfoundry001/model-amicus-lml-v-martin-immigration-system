# Model Amicus Brief — L.M.L. v. Martin

## Subtitle

The Person Already Inside the Federal Immigration System

## What is this?

This is a public model amicus brief written as if submitted in support of Plaintiffs’ Motion for Temporary Restraining Order and Preliminary Injunction in *L.M.L. v. Martin*, No. 1:26-cv-01170, in the United States District Court for the Western District of Texas, Austin Division.

It has not been filed in any court. It is not legal advice. A professional lawyer would need to adapt, verify, format, cite-check, and file any actual brief.

## Why should I care?

The brief addresses a federalism and immigration-structure problem: whether a State may create a parallel criminal-removal system that treats federally recognized immigration status, pending federal process, federal protection, federal discretion, and federally governed consequences as though they disappear when a person is encountered by state law.

## What is the claim?

The brief argues that the person is already inside the federal immigration system. Texas law must not pretend otherwise.

The central claim is that SB 4, as challenged, does not merely assist federal immigration enforcement. It creates a state criminal-removal system that substitutes a state enforcement object for the federally situated person.

## What is this not?

This is not a filed brief, legal advice, a substitute for immigration litigation practice, or a final answer to every question about immigration policy, border enforcement, federal priorities, state-federal cooperation, preemption, or public safety.

## How was this done?

The brief translates Constitutional Physiology, Structural Flow, Federalism as Scale Discipline, and Constitutional Civic Field reasoning into court-facing language. It uses person-continuity, federal legal status, process, protection, discretion, consequence, and false object substitution rather than internal theory vocabulary.
